What does the recent loss mean for the lioness'?

By Tom Garry at the Gtech Community Stadium.

Wiegman described the experience as a "really big learning for us" and insisted her team were not losing momentum, saying in her post-match press conference: "I’m not worried. I don’t worry very easily.

"Every game we get some warnings. Against Brazil we had some warnings. I don’t think we’re losing momentum, it’s fuelling, it’s 100 days to go and everyone is really excited to go to Australia. It’s just a really big learning [experience] for us."

The 53-year-old, referring to the century of days now remaining before the tournaments get under way in July, had never previously seen her England team concede more than one goal in a single game since she had taken over with the Lionesses having kept 20 clean sheets in her previous 30 matches.

In attack, it was only the third time Wiegman's England had failed to score, having only done so against the Czech Republic in a goal-less friendly in October, and against Spain in February 2022's Arnold Clark Cup. They had half-chances, with substitute Rachel Daly heading wide, but it would be hard for any of the 14,489 spectators inside the ground to deny that Australia deserved their win.
